About LCBA

Laguna College of Business and Arts (LCBA) is a respected educational institution in Laguna dedicated to providing quality education that develops intellectual competence, leadership, and social responsibility. The college prepares students to become professionals who contribute positively to society.

FOUNDER



Pedro Evangelista Llamas (1884–1950) was a dedicated educator and public servant from Pagsanjan, Laguna, who leveraged his international education from Hong Kong and the University of Michigan to transform his community. After serving a popular term as Municipal President of Pagsanjan (1916–1920), he shifted his focus to addressing the region's educational gaps by founding the Pagsanjan Academy in 1923 and the Laguna Institute in 1930. The latter was a landmark achievement, providing Calamba with its first secondary school and offering a local alternative to distant institutions in Manila or Los Baños. Despite the disruptions of World War II, Llamas’s vision endured, as the Laguna Institute survived the conflict and reopened in 1950, cementing his legacy as a pillar of Laguna’s academic development.

Vision

LCBA envisions to become an autonomous higher education institution committed to producing holistically developed globally competitive lifelong learners.

Philosophy

Inspired by Dr. Jose Rizal’s educational principles, LCBA Education upholds a balanced curriculum towards development for a better quality of life.

Mission

LCBA commits itself to the total human development of the learners through value-laden and competency-based education, policy-relevant research, and sustainable community involvement.
